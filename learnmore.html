Modelling and Simulation Hub (SA)

The Modelling and Simulation (M&S) domain is an integral part of research, product development, upscaling, and product life-cycle management across all industries, contributing to enhanced innovation and competitiveness in a global economy. Modelling and Simulation can help societies in dealing with an ever-increasing complex world that is changing rapidly, often in ways not bargained for. M&S domain allows modellers to do "what if" or virtual experiments about the world before trying them out in real-world scenarios, presenting an opportunity to explore. Currently, research and development in M&S is taking place in disparate communities considering different models and phenomena across the various fields, with considerable advancements in multiscale approaches and multidisciplinary optimisation. However, there is a lack of interoperability and integration across various model types and associated communities spanning from discrete/continuum modelling to experimental, and from science/engineering to business. .

The M&S Hub aims to facilitate and strengthen South African Modelling and Simulation R&D activities across the work domains to demonstrate their capability as tools for accelerated innovation and industrialisation. The M&S Hub focuses on four programmes:

  • Development of models and simulation methods
  • Multi-phenomena multiscale modelling and simulation technologies
  • Verification and validation
  • Data-driven modelling and simulation

    • The M&S Hub is based on a collaborative public-private partnership between the Department of Science and Innovation (DSI) and the Council for Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR). One of the key benefits of Modelling and Simulation is the ability to identify risks and time when interventions might be required, allowing decision makers to customise target-based interventions.
