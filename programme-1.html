Program 1

Models and Simulation Methods Development

Modelling and simulation solves real-world problems without any disruption or disturbances to the real-world system. It is an efficient and cost effective means to determine the impact of a variable change in the system. The analysis can be verified through scientific support and easily translated to real world systems. Modelling and simulation plays a role across all industries and disciplines by providing insights into the functioning of complex systems. Locally developed tools and solutions are envisaged to be deployed in manufacturing, chemicals, energy, and mining industries across the country. Direct economic impacts for industry include savings on the costs of experiments, faster turnaround time on the development of new complex and integrated systems, and an increase in employment rate and income. The programme was born out of the initiative to display the benefits of modelling and simulation. As a first iteration, two projects are under development, namely:

  • Project 1: Engineering Sciences Models Development
  • Project 2: Economics Sciences Models Development
