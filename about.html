Modelling and Simulation Collaboration Webtool (MSCW)

The Modelling and Simulation Collaboration is a South African network established at the CSIR and funded by the Department of Science and Innovation. It focuses on developing and applying modelling and simulation techniques across various sectors, including academic institutions, government departments, research councils, industries, and financial institutions. The collaboration ensures data preservation and open access for decision-makers to address societal challenges.

Mandate

Modelling and Simulation Collaboration is a South African integrated collaboration-network established and hosted at the CSIR and funded by the Department of Science and Innovation (DSI). This Modelling and Simulation Collaboration focuses on the development and application of modelling and simulations techniques in various sectors, academic institutions, government departments, research councils, industries, and financial institutions. We are also a Data-Driven resource hub, ensuring that existing data and knowledge is maintained by preserving it while also sharing it through open access. To address many societal challenges faced by South Africa, decision makers in government and private sector need to be the first customers for modelling and simulation technologies.

  • Develop and provide open data sharing
  • Promote and support open data policy
  • Establishing and advocating a collaboration network that promotes modelling and simulation techniques in academic institutions and industries
  • Raise the standards of best-practice in the modelling and simulation domain
  • Encourage innovation and industry-related skills while promoting modelling and simulation

    • Growth & beyond

    This Modelling and Simulation Collaboration calls for concerted efforts on building a Modelling and Simulation R&D enterprise as an integral part of Industry 4.0, particularly in South Africa. There are key cross-cutting working domains used to categorise technical hurdles encountered in Modelling and Simulation R&D, namely:

  • Development of models and simulation methods
  • Optimisation of existing methodologies
  • Decision-making and uncertainty quantification
  • Multiscale modelling tools and methods
  • Verification and validation
  • Collaboration frameworks and workflows
  • Education and training
  • Modelling and simulation data, informatics, and visualisation
  • Development of data-driven models and methods
