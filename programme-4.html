Program 4

Data-Driven Modelling and Simulation Technologies

The availability of huge volumes of data along with advancements in computational resources and data analytics are expected to have a revolutionary impact on modelling, simulation and decision making. Better use of existing data as well as availability of more standardised, structured, and validated information is fast becoming essential for the ability to reliably simulate options and make sound data-based business decisions. The vast amount of data readily available in the water, energy, mining, tourism, and anti-corruption industries make these ideal target markets for application and use of data-driven decision and support tools. The envisaged methods will facilitate the model-based integration of a variety of datasets, critical assessment of the available information, and tailored selection of future data-driven experiments. The current state-of-the-art in Machine Learning model applications in many sectors such as manufacturing, material processing, and mining industries has a significant impact on sustainability and development. R&D in machine learning model development is a rapidly increasing technology, and academics define this era the golden age of artificial intelligence and machine learning. It is envisaged that data-driven modelling and simulation technologies will be deployed and adopted by industries in the next decade, to realise the benefits brought by 4IR. Building models to imitate real-world systems, specialists can make predictions, test theories, and acquire insights that would be tough, and often impossible, to get from observations solely.
